Lively Light Ministry

EMPOWERMENT. HOPE. DIGNITY. COMMUNITY.

Lively Light Ministry has been dedicated to serving individuals and families experiencing homelessness in San Francisco since 1983. By addressing the multiple causes of homelessness through a holistic approach, we provide essential services and support. This past year, we assisted over 13,000 people, driven by our mission to help homeless and very low-income individuals obtain housing, employment, shelter, and essential services to prevent and end homelessness. Every day and night, we strive to empower our community.

Community Resources

Our community is rich with resources to help support your spiritual, emotional, and physical well-being. Below, you'll find a list of resources available to you.

  • Mental Health Services:
    • MentalHealth.gov - A federal website offering comprehensive information on mental health conditions, treatment options, and resources to educate the public and reduce stigma.
    • National Institute of Mental Health (NIMH) - The leading federal agency for mental disorders research, providing information on mental health conditions and resources for patients and families.
    • SAMHSA - A U.S. government agency that advances the nation's behavioral health, offering resources and support for mental health and substance abuse issues.
    • NAMI - The National Alliance on Mental Illness offers support groups, education, and advocacy for those affected by mental illness.
    • Anxiety and Depression Association of America (ADAA) - Dedicated to preventing, treating, and curing anxiety, depression, and related disorders through resources for individuals and professionals.
  • Housing Assistance:
    • HUD - The U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development provides housing assistance programs, including public housing, rental assistance, and resources for the homeless.
    • ShelterListings.org - An online directory of shelters and housing resources for homeless individuals and families.
    • Homeless Shelter Directory - A comprehensive directory of homeless shelters and services across the U.S.
    • Catholic Charities - Provides housing assistance, emergency shelter, and affordable housing programs to individuals and families in crisis.
    • Habitat for Humanity - Builds and renovates affordable homes for low-income families through volunteer labor and donations.
  • Other Resources:
    • VolunteerMatch - Connects volunteers with non-profit organizations to find meaningful volunteer opportunities.
    • USA.gov Disability Services - Provides information and resources for people with disabilities on a variety of topics.
    • BenefitsCheckUp - A free service from the National Council on Aging to help older adults find benefits programs that can help pay for medications, health care, food, and more.
    • 211 - A free and confidential service that helps people across North America find local resources and support for various needs.
    • CDC - The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention provides information and resources on health and safety topics.
    • Lanier Law Firm Mesothelioma - Provides legal assistance and resources for individuals affected by mesothelioma, a type of cancer caused by asbestos exposure.

If you know of other resources that should be included on this page, please contact us at livelylightministry@gmail.com.
